BoC Pay
- Mobile Payment App

Results

The revamped BoC Pay home page stands out in a competitive market due to its concise interface, user-friendly design, and wide range of features.

Users can easily navigate and locate commonly used features, resulting in faster transaction processing. Furthermore, the app eliminates repetitive or outdated content, optimizing the user experience. The addition of a recommendation section enhances user engagement by suggesting relevant features or offers.

Overall, these improvements make BoC Pay a compelling and efficient payment solution.

Overview

BoC Pay primarily focuses on its key functionality of QR code payments including transportation payments, as well as its FPS capabilities and credit card services.

Challenge

Improperly Formatted
The home page can be visually discomforting due to the presence of long lines of text at first glance. When those "Balance" are large, there is a potential problem of the lines becoming extremely long and even overlapping with the edit icon and the linked image.

Ineffective Space Utilization
There is prominently displayed outdated information regarding the "Consumption Voucher" while some frequently used features like the "Transit Code" feature are not given prominent visibility. This demonstrates a lack of efficient use of space on the home page.

Poor User Experience
Another issue is the redundancy between the features in the middle section and the navigation bar, where different names may lead to the same page such as "Scan" and "Bill Payment", ultimately causing confusion for users.

Approach

Before
After

Reorganizing Text Formatting
Collapse less important information and remove obsolete content from the home page, while expanding the text to multiple lines.

Word by word, image by image.
The layout has been simplified to create a clear and visually pleasing arrangement that avoids overwhelming users.

Before
After

Reordering Features
Remove any overlapping features such as "Scan" and "Bill Payment" that occupy space and cause confusion for users.

The position of the navigation bar aligns with users' habitual expectation of finding features and important functions. By placing Frequently used features like "Transport(Transit Code)" in the navigation bar, it provides users with convenient access to it when using the services.

Utilizing Excess Space
The "Coupons" feature has been repositioned to serve as the "Recommended" section below the "What's New" section. As a result, users can instantly view these promotional offers as they scroll down.

Progress

With the revamped BoC Pay, users can enjoy an enhanced user experience. The app's improvements not only enhance usability but also provide the organization with efficient means to promote various features and offers to users.

This combination of a better user experience and effective feature promotion makes the revamped BoC Pay a valuable solution for both users and the organization.

Design

"Balance" Display
In the first version of the "Balance" section, a text shadow was added initially to prevent it from being obscured by the background.

However, this design was discarded because of its awkward appearance. Alternatively, choosing a different background image could be a viable approach.

Navigation Bar
Initially, the plan was to remove the "Home" option from the navigation bar to make room for "Transport" or have only the four main features without including "Home."

However, it was ultimately decided to keep "Home" as it allows users to quickly navigate back to the homepage from any page without having to repeatedly use the "back" button.

"Recommended" Section
In response to current trends and users' scrolling habits on smartphones, a new "Recommended" section has been introduced below the "What's New" section to make better use of the screen space.

This change ensures that users can easily view and stay informed about the promotions and discounts available.

Gains

In the process of improving the homepage of BoC Pay, the most significant realization for me was the importance of embracing good design elements and discarding the ones that are not effective. For instance, I decided to retain the cute graphics of the page while making changes to the poor text layout. By doing so, I was able to enhance the overall visual appeal of the page.
